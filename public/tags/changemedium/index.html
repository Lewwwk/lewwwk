:::::: entry-content {.alignleft decoding=“async” height=“245” width=“163”} December 18th was the first ever StatusCamp. The event was an excellent day with over two dozen folks including the Status.net{.zem_slink rel=“homepage”} team and Peter Deitz of SocialActions{.zem_slink rel=“homepage”}. It was an amazing event to learn about where Status.net and the OpenMicroBlogging{.zem_slink rel=“homepage”} initiative (OMB) are going. The wiki has more details on OMB but it is due for a major new release and will be integrating PubSubHubBub, ActivityStrea.ms, Salmon, and WebFinger{.zem_slink rel=“homepage”}. What this means is an integration of emerging protocols to put people at the centre of micro-messaging. Not only is this a positive protocol development, but it signals the collaboration of major participants in this field to create an 0pen, interoperable system – an essential aspect of the medium of change. ...
